The Value of Determinant

Tag: Algebra Matrices
Edit   Star   Delete   Add Picture

Program Information

Name: The Value of Determinant
Domain: Numerical program
Functionality: Compute determinant of the matrices
Input: Rn,m is the set of all matrices with real elements, n rows, and m columns. A matrix A∈Rn,m with
A=⎛⎝⎜⎜a1,1⋮an,1…⋱…a1,m⋮an,m⎞⎠⎟⎟
can also be expressed by its column vectors A=(a1,⋯,am) resp. its row vectors A=(a1,⋯,an)T,where AT denotes the transposition of matrix A
Output: For a matrix A=(ai,j)1≤i,j≤n∈Rn,n let det(A)=|A| denote the determinant of A which is defined as the scalar 
det(A)=∣∣∣∣∣a1,1⋮an,1…⋱…a1,n⋮an,n∣∣∣∣∣:=∑π∈Snsgn(π)∏j=1naj,π(j)
where Sn is the permutation group on {1,⋯,n}, and sgn(π) denotes the sign of permutation π The determinant det(Ai,j) of a submatrix Ai,j∈Rn1,n1 of A∈Rn,m is called a minor of A.

Reference

An Empirical Study on the Selection of Good Metamorphic Relations https://doi.org/10.1109/COMPSAC.2006.24
