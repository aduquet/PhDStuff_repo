Detecting Faults in Technical Indicator Computations

Tag: Technical Indicators

Program Information

Name: Detecting Faults in Technical Indicator Computations
Domain: Numerical program
Functionality: Detect faults in the computation of two commonly used technical indicators, namely the moving averages (MA) and the relative strength index (RSI).
Input: To compute SMA, a minimum of n time periods of price data are required. Let t(t=k,k−1,k−2,…0) denote the time period of the prices plotted where k≥n−1. Let t = k be the oldest time period and t = 0 be the newest time period.Let P(pk,pk−1,pk−2,…,p0) be the price values from time period t=k to t=0 . In addition to the notations used to define SMA,an upward price change U(t) and a downward price change D(t) are defined i in (4) and (5) respectively for each time period,t. 
Output: SMA(n,t)={undefined 1n∑t+n−1i=tpi  t>k−n+1 k−n+1≥t≥0(1) SMA(n,t)=⎧⎩⎨⎪⎪⎪⎪undefined 1n∑t+n−1i=tpi SMA(n,t+1)−pt+nn+ptn t>k−n+1 t=k−n+1 k−n+1>t≥0(2) SSMA(n,t)=⎧⎩⎨⎪⎪undefined SMA(n,t) 1n(SMMA(n,t+1)⋅(n−1)+pt) t>k−n+1 t=k−n+1 k−n+1>t≥0(3) U(t)=⎧⎩⎨⎪⎪Undefined pt−pt+1 0 t≥k pt≥pt+1,k>t≥0 pt<pt+1,k>t≥0(4) D(t)=⎧⎩⎨⎪⎪Undefined pt+1−pt 0 t≥k pt+1≥pt,k>t≥0 pt+1≤pt,k>t≥0 (5) RSI(n,t)=⎧⎩⎨undefined 100⋅SMMA(n,U(t))SMMA(n,U(t))+SMMA(n,D(t))  t>k−n k−n≥t≥0  (6)
